Current Research

My work focuses on the intersection of high-precision astrometry and statistical inference. I develop robust Bayesian frameworks to characterize the dynamical architectures of directly imaged exoplanetary systems, utilizing the Octofitter.jl and Pigeons.jl ecosystems.

Statistical Robustness

Conducting large-scale coverage testing to quantify MCMC sampling biases in orbital models.

Keck/KPIC Refitting

Applying high-resolution spectroscopy to refine gas giant orbits with sub-milliarcsecond precision.

Gaia Bias Mitigation

Analyzing the integration of absolute astrometry to resolve mass-eccentricity degeneracies.

Beyond the Telescope

I am an advocate for structured analytical thinking across disciplines. As a classically trained pianist, arranger, and conductor, I view complex dynamical systems through a polyphonic lens—finding the "harmonics" in orbital data. My background in Linguistics and German biliteracy further informs my approach to technical communication and cross-institutional collaboration between UCSD, Caltech, and the Herzberg Institute.
